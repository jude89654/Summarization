Again bowing to Chinese pressure, Taiwan will send its chief economic planner to represent President Lee Teng-hui at November 's Asian Pacific Economic Cooperation forum summit in Kuala Lumpur, the Foreign Ministry said Friday. Lee decided to send Chiang Pin-kung to the informal leaders summit on Nov. 17-18 after `` giving much thought '' to an invitation from Malaysian Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad, the ministry said.
Presentation of an invitation to Lee by the host nation with the understanding that it will be politely refused is an annual formality that allows Taiwan to maintain a semblance of dignity and the host to avoid controversy with China.
